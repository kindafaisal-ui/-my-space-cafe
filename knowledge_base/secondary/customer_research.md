# Customer Research — My Space Target Audience

## Persona 1: Sara, The Ambitious Student
Age: 21 | University student | Needs a third place to study

Sara goes to Starbucks 3–4 times per week by default — spends 12–18 euros per session.
She goes because "it is there" — not because she loves it.

What Sara Wants:
- Fast reliable WiFi (non-negotiable)
- Power outlet at her seat (non-negotiable)
- To not feel guilty staying 3 hours on one coffee
- A space she is proud to post on Instagram
- To run into the same people — community feeling

What Sara Says:
- "I just want a place that feels like mine, you know?"
- "Starbucks is fine but it feels like I am at an airport."
- "I would pay more for a place where they actually know my order."

How Sara Discovers New Cafés:
- TikTok first — if it looks aesthetic she saves it
- Instagram when friends tag locations
- Word of mouth from other students she trusts

## Persona 2: Adam, The Young Freelancer
Age: 27 | Freelance designer | Works from cafés to escape home

Adam rotates between 3–4 local cafés with no strong loyalty.
Tried co-working spaces but they feel corporate and expensive.
Spends 15–25 euros per session.

What Adam Wants:
- A space that signals he has good taste
- Reliable WiFi for file uploads and video calls
- Slight buzz of energy — not silence, not chaos
- To feel like a regular somewhere
- To meet other creatives organically

What Adam Says:
- "I need places where the vibe actually helps me think."
- "The problem with co-working is it feels like an office."
- "Most cafés just do not feel worth posting about."

## Customer Pain Points (Survey, 18–30, n=1200)
1. Being rushed out after short time — 67%
2. Unreliable or slow WiFi — 61%
3. No power outlets at seats — 58%
4. No café feels like "my place" — 54%
5. Music too loud or random — 49%
6. Nothing worth posting about — 48%
7. Staff do not recognise regulars — 43%

My Space directly solves all 7 of these pain points.

## What Makes Young People Loyal to a Café
1. Staff remember my order or name — 71%
2. Comfortable staying as long as I want — 68%
3. Space looks good and proud to be seen there — 61%
4. Reliable WiFi — 59%
5. Where my community goes — 57%
6. Coffee quality — 49%

KEY INSIGHT: Coffee quality ranks LAST among loyalty drivers.
Young people stay loyal for community, comfort, and identity — not caffeine.
