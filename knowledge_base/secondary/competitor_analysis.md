# Competitor Analysis — My Space Café

## Competitor 1: Starbucks

### What They Do Well
- Global brand recognition and trust
- Consistent experience everywhere
- Strong loyalty app and rewards program
- Seasonal drink innovation drives social buzz

### Where They Fail Our Target (18–30)
- Impersonal at scale — 36,000 locations kills local feeling
- Designed for turnover — hard seats, loud music, 15 min average visit
- "Basic" perception — Gen Z openly mocks it on TikTok
- No intellectual or library atmosphere
- Staff turnover so high you never have "your barista"
- Generic aesthetic — nothing unique to post about

### How My Space Wins
| Category | Starbucks | My Space |
|---|---|---|
| Visit duration | 15 min | 3+ hours welcome |
| Identity signal | "I needed caffeine" | "I have taste" |
| Community feeling | None | Central to experience |
| Study-friendliness | Poor | Designed for it |
| Instagram value | Low | High |

## Competitor 2: Local Independent Cafés

### Strengths
- Authentic local character
- Personal touch from owner
- Loyal neighborhood community

### Critical Weaknesses
- No brand strategy or clear identity
- Poor visual identity — forgettable aesthetic
- Weak inconsistent social media
- No opening campaign — they just open
- Rely entirely on walk-in traffic

### How My Space Wins
We have the heart of an indie café AND the strategy of a well-funded brand.

## What Competitors Will Never Say
1. "Stay as long as you want" — competitors fear table turnover loss
2. "This is YOUR space" — no chain can say this authentically
3. Coffee + library in one concept — no major competitor owns this
